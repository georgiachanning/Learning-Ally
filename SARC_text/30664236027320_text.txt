By February 1 of each year, every school in California is required by state law to publish a School Accountability Report 
Card (SARC). The SARC contains information about the condition and performance of each California public school. Under 
the Local Contr
ol Funding Formula (LCFF) all local educational agencies (LEAs) are required to prepare a Local Control and 
Accountability Plan (LCAP), which describes how they intend to meet annual school
-
specific goals for all pupils, with 
specific activities to address
 
state and local priorities. Additionally, data reported in an LCAP is to be consistent with data 
reported in the SARC.
 
 

 
For more information about SARC requirements, see the California Department of Education (CDE) SARC web page at 
https://www.cde.ca.gov/ta/ac/sa/
.
 

 
For more information about the LCFF or LCAP, see the CDE LCFF web page at 
http
s://www.cde.ca.gov/fg/aa/lc/
.
 

 
For additional information about the school, parents/guardians and community members should contact the school 
principal or the district office.

James Guinn School opened in 1962 and serves students in grades pre
-
K through 6. About
 
fifty
-
five percent of our students 
walk to school from neighboring houses and apartments while the remainder of
 
our students are transported several 
miles to school from central Anaheim. Almost three
-
quarters of our students receive free/reduced lunches.
 
Approximately 
sixty
 
percent of our students are English learners (EL).
 
 
Guinn is a welcoming and supportive scho

all learning and growing. Our diverse population includes children from homes where over a dozen different languages 
are spoken. Thus, students represent a wide range of cultural
 
and ethnic backgrounds. Students are quite varied 
academically and physically, too. It is common to see special education students playing alongside general education 
students. In some classrooms, general education students with special needs and with one
-
to
-
one instructional assistants 
work in groups with their general education classmates. The diversity at Guinn allows us to learn from each other.
 
 
Guinn provides a rigorous and challenging instructional program that reflects high expectations for all stu
dents. We are 
focused on students meeting and exceeding mastery of state standards and helping all students become proficient in their 
grade level expectations. Instructional practices are well
-
planned and include engaging and interactive activities 
approp
riate to students' developmental levels that support the achievement of every student in our school. Coordinated 
support services are provided to students who are not yet proficient, including intervention during the school day and/or 
after school. Assessm
ent is a natural and ongoing part of the instructional process as it facilitates improvement in teaching 
and learning. A variety of supplementary programs and services ensure student success. Library/computer lab programs, 
health services, breakfast and lu
nch programs, and an after
-
school program, Anaheim Achieves, are available at Guinn. 
Additional student activities include 6th Grade Outdoor Science Education and Student Council.
 
 
The mission of James Guinn School, in partnership with the home and our com
munity, is to support the personal and 
intellectual success of every student. Our vision is to ensure that each student learns, achieving high levels of proficiency
 
and beyond.

emergency repair needs exist at this site.
