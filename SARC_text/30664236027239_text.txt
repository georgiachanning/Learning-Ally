By February 1 of each year, every school in California is required by state law to publish a School Accountability Report 
Card (SARC). The SARC contains information about the condition and performance of each California public school. Under 
the Local Contr
ol Funding Formula (LCFF) all local educational agencies (LEAs) are required to prepare a Local Control and 
Accountability Plan (LCAP), which describes how they intend to meet annual school
-
specific goals for all pupils, with 
specific activities to address
 
state and local priorities. Additionally, data reported in an LCAP is to be consistent with data 
reported in the SARC.
 
 

 
For more information about SARC requirements, see the California Department of Education (CDE) SARC web page at 
https://www.cde.ca.gov/ta/ac/sa/
.
 

 
For more information about the LCFF or LCAP, see the CDE LCFF web page at 
http
s://www.cde.ca.gov/fg/aa/lc/
.
 

 
For additional information about the school, parents/guardians and community members should contact the school 
principal or the district office.

Description and Mission Statement (School Year 2019
-
20)
 
 
Adelaide Price School was constructed in 1954 and was named for the first school nurse of the Anaheim Elementary 
School District. Adelaide is remembered fondly by the residents as a caring individua

the children of Anaheim. We continue to carry her legacy by creating an atmosphere where everyone is valued.
 
 
Mission Statement
 
Price School is a place where everyone learns and everyone teaches. Our school vision is to r
ecognize the unique value 
of each person and child, to provide an active learning environment that is both safe and supportive, and to promote a 
continuing partnership between home, school, and community. Working as a team, our mission is to provide the 
op
portunity for ALL students to acquire the necessary academic, social, emotional, and physical skills, which will 
empower them to become productive, responsible citizens of our world. At Price, we are building a better world, one 
student at a time.
 
 
Instruc
tional Program
 
The common core state standards comprise the curriculum. Course outlines, district assessment, report cards, and 
classroom practice are all designed to ensure student achievement on common core state standards. Our instructional 
focus is to 
provide a standards
-
based curriculum with assessments to support learning for all students.
 
 
Student Support Services
 
All students have access to the core curriculum through a variety of instructional programs. Mainstream English, 
 
English 
Language Mainstr
eam, and Structured English Immersion programs are offered to all students. Waivers are processed 
for parents selecting an alternative program. At Price School, we offer a Dual Language Immersion Program for students 
in K
-
6 grade. This alternative educatio
nal program enables students to learn grade level common core state standards in 
both English and Spanish. In addition, a formal English language development program is offered to all Emerging 
Bilinguals, with teachers modifying their instruction to meet t
he needs of English language proficiency levels in the 
classroom.
 
 
Student progress is monitored through formal and informal testing, review of standardized test scores, district 
assessments, and pacing charts. An academic Intervention Plan is 
developed for students in danger of retention and 
closely monitored throughout the year. Students with special needs receive the services of a speech therapist and a 
resource specialist. Students requiring the services of a self
-
contained special education
 
setting are bused to a nearby 
school. In addition, students identified as gifted and talented are given the opportunity to participate in the Gifted and 
Talented Education (GATE) Program offered at Price and throughout the District.
