School Description and Mission Statement (School Year 2019
-
20)
 
 
Burton Valley Elementary School is situated on a lovely k
noll in the Burton Valley neighborhood in the city of Lafayette. 
Forty full and part
-
time highly qualified certificated teachers and over 50 support staff serve 735 Transitional Kindergarten 
through fifth grade students. The Lafayette School District Early
 
Intervention Program for three
-
 
to five
-
year olds is also 
on our campus. The Parent Teacher Association provides a wide range of volunteer services, parent education 
opportunities and financial support. The Lafayette Partners in Education is the education
 
foundation for our District, which 
provides generous financial support to our school.
 
 
We prepare our students to be creative, responsible, productive and kind citizens. Instructional kindergarten through the 
fifth grade level is currently based on adopte
d Common Core State Standards.  Research based instructional strategies, 
including critical thinking, the workshop model, Teachers College Reading and Writing Project, and differentiated 
instruction, are all used in classrooms with a clear focus on meeting
 
the academic needs of each student. Teachers meet 
regularly in grade level teams or in cross grade level teams to engage in data
-
based inquiry, to share strategy 
implementation, and to monitor student progress. We commit to meet the needs of all students,
 
to foster continuous 
learning and to promote respect.  With outstanding teaching practices, collaboration, professional development and 
technology, we strive to create a successful learning environment for all students in a safe and nurturing environment.
